I'm a first-year student at Nanyang Technological University (NTU), Singapore, studying Chemical and Biomolecular Engineering. I hope to make a change in my programme to Computer Engineering because my interests are more aligned with said programme.

I have always loved tinkering with electronics and experimenting with software. As a kid and up to my teenage years, I played computer games in my free time. I researched and studied the vast variety of computer parts and learnt the best specifications a computer should have in order to run demanding programmes at optimal settings. I build my very own PCs from scratch, by choosing the right computer parts and assembling them together. When I built my first PC years ago, I realised just how much I enjoyed building computers. I moved on to helping my peers build their PCs. I found out that different users look for different things in their PCs; some want high-end GPUs and CPUs, while others just want something within their budgets. I learnt to look for the optimal specifications for PCs for users with different demands. I believe in incorporating our interests and passions into our work life, hence I am choosing Computer Engineering as my choice of study, since it involves me incorporating my passion, computers, into my career.

Back in National Service, I served as an administrative clerk and had multiple job scopes, one being assisting the Data Analytics branch with their work. This was when I was first introduced to the world of programming, and I observed just how much it simplifies things and how much it is used in workplaces. I was amazed by how far technology has come, and how much we can achieve using a few lines of code. I was undoubtedly intrigued, so I took the initiative to learn more. I taught myself Python, using materials I found online and inferring from code that my peers and colleagues used.
